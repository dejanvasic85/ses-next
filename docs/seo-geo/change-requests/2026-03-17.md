## B1. Switchboard Upgrades

**URL:** `/services/switchboard-upgrades`

**SEO Title:** `Switchboard Upgrades Melbourne | Licensed Electrician | SES`

**Meta Description:** `Need a switchboard upgrade in Melbourne? Licensed electricians with 19+ years experience. Safety switch installation, ceramic fuse replacement & RCD upgrades. Free quotes — call (03) 4050 7937.`

**H1:** Switchboard Upgrades Melbourne

### Body Content

Is your Melbourne home still running on an old ceramic fuse switchboard? If your safety switch keeps tripping, your lights flicker when you turn on the air conditioner, or you're planning a renovation, it's likely time for a switchboard upgrade.

Storm Electrical Solutions provides professional switchboard upgrades across Melbourne's western suburbs. With over 19 years of experience, our licensed electricians will assess your current switchboard, recommend the right upgrade, and get the job done safely — usually within a single day.

#### Why Upgrade Your Switchboard?

Older switchboards with ceramic fuses or rewireable fuses weren't designed to handle the electrical demands of modern homes. Today's households run air conditioning, solar inverters, EV chargers, induction cooktops, and multiple high-draw appliances — all of which place significantly more load on your switchboard than it was built for.

A modern switchboard with circuit breakers and residual current devices (RCDs) provides several key advantages:

- **Safety switch protection** — RCDs detect current leakage and cut power in milliseconds, preventing electrocution and reducing fire risk.
- **Circuit breakers** — Unlike ceramic fuses that blow and need replacing, circuit breakers trip and can be reset. They're faster, more reliable, and easier to manage.
- **Capacity for modern appliances** — A new switchboard can be configured with dedicated circuits for high-draw appliances like split systems, ovens, and EV chargers.
- **Compliance with current standards** — Modern switchboards meet the requirements of AS/NZS 3000 (Wiring Rules) and current Victorian electrical safety regulations.

#### Signs You Need a Switchboard Upgrade

There are several signs your switchboard may be overdue for replacement:

- Ceramic fuses or rewireable fuses still in use
- Safety switch (RCD) tripping frequently
- Flickering or dimming lights, especially when large appliances start up
- Burn marks, discolouration, or a burning smell from the switchboard
- You're planning to install solar panels, a battery, an EV charger, or air conditioning
- Your home is being renovated or you're adding new circuits
- The switchboard doesn't have enough space for additional circuits

#### Rental Property Switchboard Requirements

Since 29 March 2023, all Victorian rental properties are required to have circuit breakers and residual current devices (RCDs) installed on all power outlet and lighting circuits. This is part of the Residential Tenancies Regulations 2021.

If you're a landlord or property manager and your rental property still has an older-style switchboard, you are legally required to upgrade. Storm Electrical Solutions can assess your switchboard, carry out the upgrade, and provide the necessary compliance documentation.

#### What's Involved in a Switchboard Upgrade

A typical switchboard upgrade involves:

1. **Assessment** — We inspect your existing switchboard, check the condition of your consumer mains, and assess your current and future power needs.
2. **Quote** — We provide a clear, upfront quote with no hidden costs.
3. **Installation** — Our licensed electricians remove the old switchboard and install a new one with circuit breakers, safety switches (RCDs), and correctly labelled circuits. Power is typically off for 4–6 hours.
4. **Testing and certification** — We test all circuits, verify RCD operation, and issue a Certificate of Electrical Safety.

Most residential switchboard upgrades are completed in a single day.

#### Get a Free Quote

Every switchboard is different — the scope of work depends on the size of your home, the number of circuits, and the condition of your existing wiring. Contact us for a free, no-obligation quote so you know exactly what to expect before any work begins.

#### Service Areas

We provide switchboard upgrades across Melbourne's western and inner-western suburbs, including Altona, Altona North, Newport, Yarraville, Footscray, Williamstown, Moonee Ponds, and Ascot Vale.

### FAQ Section

**Q: How long does a switchboard upgrade take?**
A: Most residential switchboard upgrades are completed within one day. Power is typically off for 4–6 hours while the new board is installed. We'll let you know the expected timeframe before we start.

**Q: Do I need a switchboard upgrade for solar panels?**
A: In most cases, yes. Solar inverters require dedicated circuit breakers and sufficient switchboard space. If your switchboard is older or already full, an upgrade may be necessary before solar can be installed. We assess this as part of our solar consultation.

**Q: How do I know if my rental property switchboard is compliant?**
A: Since March 2023, all Victorian rental properties must have circuit breakers and RCDs on power and lighting circuits. If your switchboard still has ceramic or rewireable fuses, it's not compliant. Contact us for an assessment — we'll advise what's needed and provide a quote.

## B2. Mains Upgrades

**URL:** `/services/mains-upgrades`
**Sanity slug:** `mains-upgrades`

**SEO Title:** `Mains Power Upgrades Melbourne | Licensed Electrician | SES`

**Meta Description:** `Mains power upgrade in Melbourne? Our licensed electricians upgrade consumer mains cables, single to 3-phase power & overhead to underground supply. Free quotes — call (03) 4050 7937.`

**H1:** Mains Power Upgrades Melbourne

### Body Content

If your home's electrical supply can't keep up with modern demands — tripping circuits, dimming lights, or an inability to add new appliances — you may need a mains power upgrade.

Storm Electrical Solutions provides mains upgrade services across Melbourne's western suburbs. Our licensed electricians assess your current supply, coordinate with your electricity distributor, and carry out the upgrade safely and to Australian Standards.

#### What Is a Mains Upgrade?

A mains upgrade involves replacing or upgrading the electrical supply cables that connect your property's switchboard (meter box) to the street power supply — either an overhead power pole or an underground supply pit.

Most Melbourne homes have a 63-amp single-phase supply, which is sufficient for standard modern usage. However, older homes may still have a 32-amp or 40-amp supply, which is no longer adequate for the electrical load most households now require.

#### When Do You Need a Mains Upgrade?

Common reasons for a mains upgrade include:

- **Older home with a small supply** — Homes with 32A or 40A supplies often can't handle modern appliances like ducted air conditioning, induction cooktops, or electric hot water systems without overloading.
- **Adding solar or a battery** — Solar inverters and battery systems add load to your supply. An undersized mains cable can limit what you can install.
- **Installing an EV charger** — Level 2 EV chargers draw significant current. Your existing supply may not have the capacity.
- **Renovating or extending** — Adding rooms, a granny flat, or a workshop often means you need a larger electrical supply to support new circuits.
- **Upgrading to three-phase power** — Some equipment (commercial machinery, large air conditioning systems, workshops) requires three-phase power instead of single-phase.
- **Old or deteriorating cables** — Homes over 40–50 years old may have cotton-insulated or rubber-insulated mains cables that no longer meet safety standards and need replacing.

#### Types of Mains Upgrades

**Single-phase upgrade:** Increasing your existing single-phase supply from 32A or 40A to the standard 63A. This is the most common residential mains upgrade.

**Three-phase upgrade:** Installing a three-phase supply for higher power requirements. This involves coordination with your electricity distributor (e.g., Jemena, CitiPower, Powercor) and may require a new meter.

**Overhead to underground conversion:** Replacing an overhead mains connection with an underground supply. This improves reliability and removes the exposure to storm damage or tree contact.

#### What's Involved

1. **Assessment** — We inspect your existing mains cables, switchboard, and metering setup to determine what upgrade is needed.
2. **Distributor coordination** — We handle the application and coordination with your electricity distributor (Jemena, CitiPower, or Powercor) for supply changes, meter upgrades, or new connections.
3. **Installation** — Our electricians install new consumer mains cables between your switchboard and the point of supply. If a switchboard upgrade is also needed, we do both at the same time.
4. **Testing and certification** — All work is tested to AS/NZS 3000 and a Certificate of Electrical Safety is issued.

#### Get a Free Quote

The scope of a mains upgrade depends on the type of upgrade, cable length, and whether overhead or underground work is involved. Every property is different, so contact us for a detailed, upfront quote after an initial assessment.

#### Service Areas

We provide mains upgrades across Melbourne's western and inner-western suburbs, including Altona, Altona North, Newport, Yarraville, Footscray, Williamstown, Moonee Ponds, and Ascot Vale.

### FAQ Section

**Q: How long does a mains upgrade take?**
A: Most single-phase mains upgrades are completed in one day. Three-phase conversions or underground supply work may take longer depending on distributor timelines and the scope of civil works. We'll give you a clear timeline after the initial assessment.

**Q: Do I need to be home during a mains upgrade?**
A: Someone needs to be at the property to provide access to the switchboard and meter. Power will be off for several hours during the upgrade, so we recommend planning accordingly.

**Q: Can you upgrade my mains and switchboard at the same time?**
A: Yes, and this is actually the most common scenario. Many homes that need a mains upgrade also have an older switchboard that should be replaced. Doing both at the same time is more cost-effective and means you only have one day of downtime.

## B3. Electrical Installation Report (Residential Tenancy Act)

**URL:** `/services/electrical-installation-report`
**Sanity slug:** `electrical-installation-report`

**SEO Title:** `Electrical Safety Check for Rental Properties Melbourne | SES`

**Meta Description:** `Need an electrical installation report for your Melbourne rental property? Licensed electricians providing safety checks to AS/NZS 3019 — compliant with the Residential Tenancies Act. Call (03) 4050 7937.`

**H1:** Electrical Installation Report — Rental Property Safety Checks Melbourne

### Body Content

Under Victorian law, all rental properties must have an electrical safety check carried out every two years by a licensed electrician. Storm Electrical Solutions provides compliant electrical installation reports for landlords and property managers across Melbourne's western suburbs.

#### What Is an Electrical Installation Report?

An Electrical Installation Report is a formal written document produced by a licensed electrician after inspecting and testing the electrical installations, fittings, and appliances in a rental property. The report covers the condition of the electrical system and identifies any safety issues or required repairs.

This report is required under the Residential Tenancies Regulations 2021, which mandate regular electrical safety checks for all Victorian rental properties.

#### Who Needs This?

If you own or manage a rental property in Victoria, you are legally required to arrange electrical safety checks. The regulations apply to:

- All new rental agreements signed on or after 29 March 2021
- Existing fixed-term agreements of more than five years that roll over into periodic tenancy after 29 March 2021

As the rental provider (landlord), you must keep records of safety checks and provide the date of the most recent check to your tenant on request.

#### What We Check

Our electrical safety check is carried out in accordance with Section 4 of AS/NZS 3019:2022 (Electrical Installations — Periodic Assessment) and covers:

- **Switchboard condition** — Inspection of circuit breakers, safety switches (RCDs), labelling, and overall board condition
- **Wiring and cables** — Assessment of accessible wiring for damage, deterioration, or non-compliant installations
- **Power points and switches** — Testing of all accessible power points for correct polarity, earth continuity, and insulation resistance
- **Lighting circuits** — Verification that lighting circuits are properly protected and functioning safely
- **Safety switches (RCDs)** — Testing of all RCDs for correct trip time and functionality
- **Smoke alarm integration** — Visual check that smoke alarms are present and connected (dedicated smoke alarm compliance checks are a separate service)
- **General safety assessment** — Identification of any visible hazards, overloaded circuits, or non-compliant installations

#### What You Receive

After the inspection, we provide a comprehensive written report that includes:

- The date of the safety check
- Details of all areas covered by the check
- Any areas not covered and the reason (e.g., inaccessible)
- Results of testing, including any faults or safety issues identified
- Recommendations for repairs or further investigation
- The name and licence number of the electrician who conducted the check

This report meets the record-keeping requirements under the Residential Tenancies Regulations 2021. If any repair work is carried out, we also issue a Certificate of Electrical Safety (COES) as required by Energy Safe Victoria.

#### Switchboard Compliance for Rental Properties

Since 29 March 2023, all Victorian rental properties are required to have:

- Circuit breakers compliant with AS/NZS 3000
- Residual current devices (RCDs/safety switches) on all power outlet and lighting circuits, compliant with AS/NZS 3190, AS/NZS 61008.1, and AS/NZS 61009.1

If your rental property's switchboard doesn't meet these requirements, we can carry out the necessary upgrades as part of the same visit. See our [Switchboard Upgrades](/services/switchboard-upgrades) page for more information.

#### How Often Is It Required?

Electrical safety checks must be conducted every two years. If a check hasn't been done in the last two years at the time a tenant moves in, the landlord must arrange one as soon as practicable.

We recommend scheduling checks proactively rather than waiting for a tenancy changeover, to avoid delays and ensure ongoing compliance.

#### For Property Managers

We work with property managers across Melbourne's western suburbs and can coordinate inspections directly with tenants. We provide digital reports for easy record-keeping and are happy to set up recurring two-year reminders for your managed properties.

#### Service Areas

We provide electrical installation reports and safety checks across Melbourne's western and inner-western suburbs, including Altona, Altona North, Newport, Yarraville, Footscray, Williamstown, Moonee Ponds, and Ascot Vale.

### FAQ Section

**Q: How much does an electrical installation report cost for a rental property?**
A: The cost depends on the size of the property and the number of circuits, but a standard residential electrical safety check typically takes 1–2 hours. Contact us for a quote — we offer competitive rates for property managers with multiple properties.

**Q: What happens if faults are found during the safety check?**
A: We document all faults in the report and advise the landlord or property manager on what repairs are needed. If you'd like us to carry out the repairs, we can often do so during the same visit for minor issues. For larger jobs like a switchboard upgrade, we'll provide a separate quote. Any electrical work completed will include a Certificate of Electrical Safety.

**Q: Is the electrical safety check the same as a Certificate of Electrical Safety (COES)?**
A: No. They are different documents. The Electrical Installation Report is the record of the periodic safety check required under the Residential Tenancies Regulations. A Certificate of Electrical Safety is a separate document issued when electrical repair, maintenance, or installation work is carried out. You may need both — the report after the inspection, and a COES if repairs are performed.
