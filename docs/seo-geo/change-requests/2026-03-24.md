### Content Structure

**H1:** Emergency Electrician Melbourne — Fast Same-Day Response

**H2: What Counts as an Electrical Emergency**

- Power outage to the whole house or specific circuits
- Sparking or arcing from outlets, switches, or the switchboard
- Burning smell from wiring or electrical fittings
- Safety switch tripping repeatedly and won't reset
- Exposed or damaged wiring (storm damage, accidental damage, rodent damage)
- Water near live electrical components (flooding, burst pipes)

**H2: How We Handle Urgent Electrical Work**

- CRITICAL FRAMING: SES does NOT offer 24/7 or after-hours callouts. Be honest about this.
- Frame as: "fast same-day response during business hours" and "priority booking for urgent electrical issues"
- Explain that during business hours (Mon–Fri 7am–6pm), urgent calls are prioritised and we aim to get to you the same day
- For genuine out-of-hours emergencies (smell of burning, active sparking), advise calling 000 or the electricity distributor's fault line
- This honesty builds trust — don't pretend to be a 24/7 service

**H2: Common Emergency Scenarios We Handle**

- Safety switch won't stop tripping — diagnose and fix the fault
- Complete power loss — isolate whether it's a supply issue or internal fault
- Storm damage to electrical systems — make safe and repair
- Switchboard failures — emergency replacement or repair
- Faulty wiring causing sparks or burning smell — isolate and repair
- Brief practical description of each, not just a list

**H2: What to Do Before the Electrician Arrives**

- Practical safety advice: switch off at the main, don't touch anything wet/sparking, keep clear of the switchboard
- When to call 000 vs when to call an electrician
- This is genuinely useful content that AI models love to cite

**H2: Service Areas**

- List the service areas

- Note that response time depends on current workload and location

### FAQs (3, with FAQ schema)

1. **How quickly can you respond to an electrical emergency?**
   Answer: During business hours (Mon–Fri 7am–6pm), we prioritise urgent calls and aim for same-day response across Melbourne's western suburbs. Response time depends on current workload and your location. For life-threatening emergencies outside business hours — like active sparking, fire, or flooding near electrics — call 000 immediately.

2. **What should I do if my safety switch keeps tripping?**
   Answer: Start by unplugging all appliances on the affected circuit and try resetting the safety switch. If it holds, plug appliances back in one at a time to find the faulty one. If the switch trips with everything unplugged, the fault is likely in the wiring itself and needs a licensed electrician to diagnose. Don't keep resetting a tripping safety switch without finding the cause.

3. **Do you offer 24/7 emergency electrical services?**
   Answer: No — we operate during business hours, Monday to Friday, 7am to 6pm. Within those hours, we offer priority same-day response for urgent electrical issues. If you have a genuine emergency outside business hours (smell of burning, active sparking, or flooding near electrical systems), call 000 or your electricity distributor's 24-hour fault line.
